# Anti-Pattern: Vague Instructions

## What it is

Vague instructions occur when a prompt does not clearly define:
- Role
- Goal
- Context
- Output format

This leads to inconsistent or low-quality outputs.

---

## Example of a bad prompt

"Help me write something about marketing."

---

## Why this fails

- No defined audience
- No content type specified
- No tone or style guidance
- No structure for output
- Too open-ended for consistent results

---

## Improved version

You are a marketing specialist.

Write a 150-word social media post promoting a new fitness app.

Requirements:
- Target audience: young professionals
- Tone: motivational and energetic
- Include a call to action
- End with 3 relevant hashtags

---

## Why the improved version works

- Clear role definition
- Specific output format
- Defined audience
- Controlled tone
- Measurable constraints

---

## Key takeaway

The more precise the instruction, the more reliable the output.

Vagueness is the most common cause of poor LLM responses.
